1.Market Highlights

The Nepal Stock Exchange (NEPSE) has demonstrated a positive trend in recent sessions. On February 17, 2025, the NEPSE index rose by 29.87 points (1.10%) to close at 2,727.91, with a total turnover of Rs. 10.04 billion. The following day, February 18, 2025, the index continued its upward trajectory, gaining 11.91 points (0.43%) to settle at 2,739.82, and a turnover of Rs. 10.43 billion.

  • “It's really the perfect setup for Netflix": Geetha Ranganathan, a senior analyst at Bloomberg, flagged the content lineup from the quarter as a key driver for likely higher subscriber retention, along with new additions.
  • Ad revenue contribution small, but growing: Investors should watch out for signs of advertising growth, with the ad tier now having over 70 million global monthly active users. The company plans to launch its first-party ad server across all markets in 2025.

2.Top Performers

Himalayan Reinsurance Limited (HRL) led the market in turnover on February 18, 2025, with transactions amounting to Rs. 820.2 million, closing at Rs. 936 per share. Notably, four scrips achieved a 10% increase, hitting their upper circuit limits.

3.Sector Overview

The 'Others' sector experienced the most significant gain, advancing by 2.47%, while the 'Trading' sector faced a decline of 1.27%.

4.Investor Insights

Despite the overall positive market movement, investors are advised to exercise caution. Recent reports highlight that over 26% of companies listed on NEPSE are loss-making, yet their share prices remain disproportionately high due to potential market manipulation. This underscores the importance of thorough research and due diligence before making investment decisions.

5.Upcoming Events

Upper Hewakhola Hydropower Company has proposed issuing 100% right shares, pending approval from the Electricity Regulatory Commission. Additionally, Crest Micro Life Insurance Limited is set to issue 1,845,000 units of IPO shares to the general public from February 22 to 26, 2025.
